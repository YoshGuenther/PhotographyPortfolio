Creative Distractions

October 20th, 2015

How easily distracted are you? Lately, I will be working and let my thoughts drift off when I notice a small scene or item.

This originally began as I was walking to class and noticed a geometrically pleasing fence post. I walked past this post every day and would let my mind drift off. With each visitation, I saw it from a different perspective and when I finally did decide to shoot it, I had thought about it enough that I knew to shoot from a height right above the top of the post.

However, I am getting off topic. While I am able to focus very well on my work, I have learned to let myself get distracted because it helps my creativity flow. For example, last night I was going about cleaning my lenses. I placed my telephoto lens underneath a lamp to help identify the spots on the front face and noticed that the light bulb was reflecting back in an array of pastel colors. Instantly, I put away my cleaning gear and setup to shoot the unique reflecting.

When I stumble into creative ideas like this, I find it best to experiment. I adjusted the angle that the telephoto lens was positioned and the distance between the lamp and the lens. Ultimately, I developed a combination that filled the telephoto lens with the maximum number light bulbs. Before I wrapped things up, I tried to manually illuminate the bulb by turning the lamp off and pointing a flash at it. It was surprisingly effective as the flash also created a pastel colored reflection in the lens.

All the while, time was slipping by and it was not until about 11:00 pm before I was satisfied with my shots. While I did not get all my camera lenses cleaned, I became more willing to let my mind drift and thus, boost my creative ability.
